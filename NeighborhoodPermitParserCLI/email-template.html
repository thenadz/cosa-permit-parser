I'm Dan Rossiter, the current president of Thunderbird Hills Neighborhood Association. What follows is my attempt to simplify our lives as neighborhood leaders when it comes to reviewing permit activity within our neighborhoods. While the City of San Antonio provides weekly emails linking to active permits, the information is not personalized to your neighborhood, making it impossible to quickly find just the permits within the bounds of your neighborhood.

I finally got tired of this, so I put together this tool to take all of the tens of thousands of permits across the city and find just the ones within Thunderbird Hills. Once I had this for myself, I thought it might be useful for you too so here it is. Any and all feedback would be greatly appreciated. If you receive information for permits that looks wrong or you think of a way to make the information more useful, I'm all ears! Do note that the tool is only as good as the data provided by the city, which is sometimes lacking.

Attached you will find a CSV file containing the ###COUNT permit applications reported by the city within ###NEIGHBORHOOD since the beginning of the calendar year.
